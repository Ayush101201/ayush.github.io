Jones Road Beauty · Homepage Redesign · 2025
Overview

A brand that deserved
a better first impression.

Jones Road Beauty is a clean, high-performance cosmetics brand founded by Bobbi Brown. Despite a strong product lineup and loyal customer base, the homepage suffered from usability issues quietly costing conversions — poor visual hierarchy, accessibility gaps, and a layout working against natural scanning patterns.

This project was a hiring test assignment: analyze the existing homepage, identify friction points, and redesign it to better reflect the brand's quality and improve the user experience end-to-end.

Primary Goal

"Increase conversion rate by improving clarity, hierarchy, and trust across the homepage."

Scope

Heuristic Audit ✓
Homepage Redesign ✓
Design Annotations ✓
Decision Documentation ✓
Research & Analysis

What was
broken.

I audited every section of the original homepage against usability heuristics, accessibility standards (WCAG), and e-commerce conversion best practices.

  • 01

    Offer Strip Ignored

    Placed at the very top in a low-contrast bar, the free shipping offer sat outside users' natural scanning zones — consistently overlooked.

  • 02

    Non-Standard Bag Icon

    The cart icon used a non-standard visual metaphor, violating the recognition over recall heuristic. Users struggled to identify it at a critical conversion touchpoint.

  • 03

    Featured Products — No Visual Separation

    All products displayed on the same flat background without boundaries. Poor spacing caused misclicks and made individual products hard to distinguish.

  • 04

    Shop by Collection — Accessibility Gaps

    Small font sizes reduced readability. Zero spacing between images disrupted eye movement and increased cognitive load.

  • 05

    Missing Context & Weak CTAs

    Multiple sections lacked clear headings. CTAs were small, low-contrast, and easy to miss — failing to guide users toward the next action.

  • 06

    Footer Hierarchy Collapse

    All footer elements carried equal visual weight. Poor contrast and inconsistent typography reduced both readability and brand consistency.

Design Decisions

Every change had
a reason.

The redesign wasn't about making things look nicer — it was about solving real usability problems with deliberate, documented decisions.

Navigation

Standard Cart Icon

Replaced the ambiguous bag icon with the universally recognized shopping cart symbol, aligning with users' mental models and reducing confusion at a key conversion touchpoint.

Offer Strip

Repositioned Below Hero

Moved the free shipping offer below the hero image to sit within users' natural scanning path, with updated color and contrast for better visibility.

Information Architecture

Categories Surfaced Early

Repositioned Shop by Category higher on the page with a card-based layout. Clear boundaries and labels reduce cognitive load and support faster discovery.

Product Discovery

Tabbed Product Navigation

Introduced a tabbed component (Makeup / Skincare / Haircare) to organize featured products. Hover-state add-to-cart reduces clicks and streamlines the path to purchase.

Social Proof

New Reviews Section

Added a dedicated customer reviews section. Real user voices build credibility, validate purchasing decisions, and create a sense of community.

Trust Signals

Secure Payments · Easy Returns

Three trust badges placed strategically near the bottom reduce purchase hesitation at the exact point users decide whether to buy.

Engagement

Products in Action (Video)

A new video section shows real people using the products, building trust through authentic visual proof and strengthening emotional connection with the brand.

Accessibility

Headings + Font Sizes

Clear descriptive headings and larger, higher-contrast text across all sections. Every section now has an orientation cue, meeting WCAG readability standards.

AWS · Landing Page UX Redesign · 2026
Overview

The world's biggest cloud,
with a homepage that
loses users.

Amazon Web Services is the dominant force in cloud computing — 200+ services, used by millions of developers, startups, and enterprise teams worldwide. Yet its landing page fails almost every core UX principle: cognitive overload in the hero, no clear value proposition, a navigation architecture that overwhelms instead of guides, and mobile performance that lags far behind every major rival.

This was a self-initiated 14-day UX case study: audit the current homepage, benchmark it against Google Cloud, Azure, and DigitalOcean, identify the friction points costing AWS conversions — and propose a redesign strategy grounded in research, not taste.

Central Question

"How might AWS's homepage better serve each user type — from the student exploring cloud for the first time to the enterprise CTO evaluating migration?"

Research & Analysis

What the audit
revealed.

I audited every major section of the AWS homepage against Nielsen's 10 heuristics, WCAG accessibility standards, and conversion best practices — then scored each problem by severity.

  • 01
    Critical

    Cognitive Overload in the Hero

    Multiple competing headlines, an auto-rotating carousel, and simultaneous CTAs fight for attention. No single dominant narrative — users process nothing before it disappears.

    Heuristic #8 — Aesthetic & Minimalist Design

  • 02
    Critical

    Navigation Architecture is Overwhelming

    200+ products exposed in a mega-menu with no filtering by need or expertise. No visible "Getting Started" for new users. Search is buried rather than prominent.

    Heuristic #6 — Recognition Over Recall

  • 03
    High

    No Value Proposition Above the Fold

    AWS's hero doesn't answer "What does this do, and why me?" The Free Tier — the most powerful conversion tool — sits below the fold. Zero social proof is visible on load.

    Heuristic #2 — Match System & Real World

  • 04
    High

    Poor Visual Hierarchy & Typography

    Flat font-size distribution, WCAG contrast failures in banners, dense text blocks with insufficient whitespace. CTAs of different importance are styled near-identically.

    Heuristic #8 — Aesthetic & Minimalist Design

  • 05
    High

    No Audience-Oriented Entry Points

    Unlike Google Cloud and Azure, there is no "I am a…" segmentation. A student developer and an enterprise CTO see the exact same undifferentiated page — no use-case discovery paths exist.

    Heuristic #7 — Flexibility & Efficiency of Use

  • 06
    High

    Mobile is an Afterthought

    Over 40% of first-touch traffic arrives on mobile. The mega-menu collapses poorly, images break on portrait orientation, and tap targets fall below accessibility minimums. Lighthouse mobile score: ~62.

    Heuristic #5 — Error Prevention

Redesign Strategy

Six problems.
Six targeted solutions.

Every proposed change maps directly to a diagnosed problem. No decision was made for aesthetics alone — each one is anchored in research, heuristics, or competitor evidence.

Hero Section

Single-Focus Hero

One bold, benefit-driven headline replaces the cluttered carousel. One dominant CTA. A layout that communicates its core message in under 3 seconds — the attention span of a first-time visitor trying to evaluate a platform.

Navigation

Audience-First Navigation

The nav is restructured around user needs — not product categories. Three clear pathways: New to Cloud, Developer, and Enterprise Buyer. Each pathway surfaces only what that specific user needs to see.

Information Architecture

Progressive Disclosure

Not all 200+ services belong on the homepage. The redesign shows just enough for users to self-identify their path, then routes them deeper into relevant product pages with full confidence in where they're heading.

Visual System

Design System Upgrade

A refined typographic scale and accessibility-compliant color system that maintains AWS brand identity while dramatically improving scannability, contrast ratios, and conversion hierarchy across the page.

Segmentation

Audience Segmentation Tabs

Hero-level tabs that dynamically update the headline, sub-copy, and CTAs based on user identity. When users see themselves in the page, perceived relevance and time-on-site both increase — proven by Google Cloud and Azure.

Accessibility

Mobile-First Redesign

Start from a 375px viewport and scale up — reversing the current desktop-down approach. Over 40% of first-touch visits are mobile; the redesign treats this as the primary experience, not a secondary consideration.

Reflections

What I learned.

  • 01

    Complexity is a failure, not a feature — 200+ services are a marketing strength but a UX liability when front-loaded onto a homepage. A homepage's job is to help each user find their one thing, fast.

  • 02

    Segmentation multiplies conversion — when users see themselves in a page, they engage more deeply and convert at higher rates. Audience tabs aren't UX niceties; they're direct, measurable revenue levers.

  • 03

    The best design is invisible — the goal was never to make AWS look dramatically different. It was to remove friction so completely that users stop noticing the interface and start experiencing the value of AWS itself.

  • 04

    Data validates; empathy inspires — every decision is grounded in heuristic evaluation or competitive benchmarking. But the creative direction came from genuine empathy — sitting inside each persona's frustration and asking "what would actually help right now?"

HUZZ · Personality-First Dating · 2026
Overview

The dating app problem
is a design problem.

Modern dating apps prioritize visual attraction while overlooking personality, values, and genuine compatibility. Users face friction at every step: confusing onboarding, shallow matching, unsafe interactions, and interfaces that feel generic and cold.

HUZZ addresses this gap by reimagining the dating app from first principles. An energetic, personality-first design with bold branding, frictionless onboarding, and safety-conscious features creates a platform where users feel both attracted and understood.

This self-initiated case study documents the end-to-end product design — from persona development through complete app flow with 28 unique screens, design system, and measurable impact strategy.

Central Question

"How might we design a dating app that values personality equally with appearance, creating meaningful connections while maintaining user safety and control?"

Research & Analysis

Why dating apps
are failing users.

I analyzed market-leading dating apps (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, Match) against usability heuristics, accessibility standards, and user satisfaction metrics. The research revealed consistent friction points costing engagement, safety, and retention.

  • 01
    Critical

    Personality is invisible until matching

    Most apps show photos first, hobbies buried. Users swipe blind on values and lifestyle, leading to incompatible matches and wasted conversations.

  • 02
    Critical

    Safety mechanisms are reactive, not proactive

    Report buttons exist but feel like bandages. No tools to prevent ghosting, inappropriate behavior, or uncomfortable situations before they escalate.

  • 03
    High

    Onboarding is friction, not delight

    Long flows with permission requests, unclear steps, and multiple form screens. 40% of users abandon during signup. Low completion = incomplete profiles = poor matching quality.

  • 04
    High

    Visual design is interchangeable

    Purple gradients, generic UI, safe-but-boring. Dating is emotional—apps should feel energetic, bold, and human, not corporate.

  • 05
    High

    Matching logic ignores values & lifestyle

    Algorithm is photo-first, with minimal personality weight. Two users with identical values but different looks are treated as incomparable.

  • 06
    High

    Premium tier feels mandatory, not optional

    Free experience is crippled. Swipe limits, match limits, message limits. Users feel squeezed, not served.

User Research

Who are HUZZ
users really?

Research identified three distinct user types with overlapping but distinct needs. Each persona drives specific design decisions throughout the app.

Persona 01

The Intentional Seeker

26–35, looking for a real relationship. Values alignment is non-negotiable. Currently frustrated with shallow matching. Needs personality-first discovery and confidence that time won't be wasted on incompatible matches.

Persona 02

The Explorer

18–28, open to connections but no rush. Wants a fun, energetic experience. Willing to try new apps if the vibe feels right. Needs friction-free onboarding and an interface that feels fresh, not generic.

Persona 03

The Safety-First User

25–45, previous negative experiences with apps. Prioritizes respect, verification, and clear reporting channels. Needs reassurance that their boundaries will be respected and unwanted behavior addressed quickly.

Cross-Cutting Need

Inclusivity & Representation

All personas need to feel welcomed, not tokenized. Non-binary, trans, LGBTQ+, and culturally diverse users must find authentic pathways without excessive disclosure.

Design Strategy

Six problems.
Six targeted solutions.

Every screen and feature maps directly to a diagnosed problem. Design decisions are anchored in research, persona needs, and competitive differentiation — not aesthetics alone.

Problem 01

Personality-First Matching

Profile cards surface hobbies, interests, values, and religion equally with photos. Users discover compatibility on what matters before swiping, reducing mismatches and wasted conversations.

Problem 02

Frictionless Onboarding

Seven-step flow that feels like discovery, not bureaucracy. Phone verification → Profile info → Photos → Interests → Bio → Identity verification → Ready. Each step is optional or pleasurable, not mandatory busywork.

Problem 03

Proactive Safety Architecture

Unmatch, report, and ghosting acknowledgment built into every interaction. Users maintain full control at all times. Safety is designed in, not bolted on.

Problem 04

Bold Visual Identity

Pink and red palette reflects passion and emotional authenticity. Energetic but professional. Memorable and distinctive, not interchangeable with competitors.

Problem 05

Premium Done Right

Unlimited super-likes and profile boost are genuine upgrades, not artificial limits. Free users can still discover matches and message freely. Premium tier feels like a choice, not a trap.

Problem 06

Real-Time Connection Features

Messaging with status indicators, compliment ice-breakers, and instant reporting. Makes conversations feel safer and more intentional. Reduces unsolicited harassment and awkward silences.

Reflections

What this project revealed.

  • 01

    Personality-driven matching is table stakes. Users are tired of shallow swiping. Apps that treat photos and personality as equally important will win loyalty in a commoditized market. Design that surfaces values equals design that converts.

  • 02

    Safety is a brand differentiator. Most apps treat safety as compliance. HUZZ treats it as core brand promise. Proactive features (ghosting acknowledgment, easy reporting) aren't nice-to-haves — they're retention engines.

  • 03

    Onboarding speed compounds across the product. Quick, smooth signup leads to filled-out profiles, which leads to better matching, which leads to longer sessions. Friction at step 1 echoes through every metric. Every screen shaved from the flow is ROI.

  • 04

    Emotional design sells what features can't. Bold pink, energetic vibe, and human language build brand affinity faster than feature parity. Dating apps live or die on feeling. Design that feels human wins over design that feels optimized.
